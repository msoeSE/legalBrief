What is an Appellant's Initial Brief?

  • An Appellant is the party who is appealing a decision by the circuit court (also called the trial court) and who wants the Court of Appeals to reverse all or part of the decision.
  • An Initial Brief is a formal document filed by the Appellant. It explains why the circuit court decided your case incorrectly.
  • An Initial Brief presents the facts of the case, describes the circuit court's decision, cites to legal authorities such as statutes and case law, and explains how the circuit court erred in your case.
  • The rules governing the form, contents and filing of an Appellant's Initial Brief are found in Wis. Stat. §809.19.
