Welcome to the Brief Assistant!

The Brief Assistant WILL:

  • Show you an example of and Appellant's Initial Brief for the Wisconsin Court of Appeals
  • Explain the different sections of an Appellant's Initial Brief.
  • Direct you to websites where you can find cases, statutes, and other forms of law to cite in your Appellant's Initial Brief.
  • Help you create a draft of a correctly-formatted Appellant's Initial Brief that you can download,save,fill out, edit, and then file in your appeal.

The Brief Assistant will NOT:

  • Generate a completed Appellant's Initial Brief for you. You will need to fill out the sections of the Appellant's Initial Brief and complete additional steps before you can file it with the Wisconsin Court of Appeals.
  • Do legal research or tell you what law applies to your case.
  • Read Wis. Stat. §809.19 for you. Users are responsible for reading and complying with §809.19, which governs the form, contents and filing of appellate briefs.
  • Generate other forms that must be filed in an appeal--such as a Notice of Appeal or a Statement on Transcript.

Disclaimer:

The Brief Assistant does NOT provide legal advice. The Appellate Practice Section strongly urges parties to an appeal to retain a lawyer to represent them. Sometimes that is not possible.

So if you have filed a civil appeal and you cannot afford a lawyer, you may wish to call, text, or email the Appellate Help Desk at 414-671-9041 or appellatehelp@gmail.com for advice on how to prepare your brief.

If you have filed a criminal appeal and you cannot afford a lawyer, you may be eligible for one through the Wisconsin State Public Defender. For criminal appeals in Milwaukee, call 414-227-4805. For criminal appeals in all other parts of Wisconsin, call 608-266-3440.
