For information on the procedures for preparing and filing briefs in the Wisconsin Court of Appeals:

Read Wis. Stat. §809.19 which governs the form and filing of appellate briefs.

Review the Wisconsin Court of Appeals’ Guide to Appellate Procedure for the Self-Represented.

Contact the Appellate Help Desk.

To find legal authorities to cite in your Initial Brief, Response Brief, or Reply Brief, try these free online resources:

To find a broad range of legal resources and to "ask a librarian" visit the Wisconsin State Law Library's website.

To find statues, constitutions, and administrative rules, visit the Wisconsin State Legislature's website.

To find case law, visit Google Scholar, select "case law" and the appropriate court(s). You can also visit the Public Library of Law.

To search for law review articles about specific areas of law, try Law Technology Today.

To search a broad range of legal resources with one query, visit DRAGNET.

To get brief answers from a lawyer about the law that applies to the issues in civil cases, visit Wisconsin Free Legal Answers.
