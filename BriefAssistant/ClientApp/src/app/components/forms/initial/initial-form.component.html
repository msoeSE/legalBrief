Brief Information

Issues presented is required.
This section briefly describes the question(s) that you want the Court of Appeals to decide. Usually, each Issue Presented will identify a mistake that the circuit court made in your case. The Issues section is required by Wis. Stat. §809.19(1)(b) .
Oral argument statement is required.
The Statement on Oral Argument section explains whether the Court of Appeals should hold a hearing where you and your opponent are allowed to speak. Usually, the Court of Appeals does not hold oral argument. Instead, it decides an appeal after reading the parties' briefs. The factors that the Court of Appeals considers in deciding whether to hold oral argument appear in Wis. Stat. §809.22 . The statement on Oral Argument section is required by Wis. Stat. §809.19(1)(c) .
Publication statement is required.
The Statement on Publication section states whether you think the Court of Appeals should publish the decision in your case by printing it in a book that contains important decisions. The factors that the Court of Appeals considers in deciding whether to publish its decision appear in Wis. Stat. §809.23 . The Statement on Publication section is required by Wis. Stat.§809.19(1)(c) .
Case and facts statement is required.
The Statement of Case and Fact section describes the nature of the case and provides a short overview of what happened at the circuit court level. It also presents the relevant facts - i.e only those facts needed to understand and decide your arguments. Write your Statement of Case and Facts in chronological order. You may only refer to facts that were previously presented as evidence to the circuit court; you may not present new evidence in your brief. Each statement of fact must be followed by a citation to the Record on Appeal. For example, "R.35:26" is a citation to Item No. 35 at page 26 in the Record on Appeal. The Statement of Case and Facts section is required by Wis. Stat.§809.19(1)(d). Please note that for certain appeals, the law requires appellate briefs to refer to the parties and other individuals by initials, an appropriate pseudonym, or other designation. See Wis. Stat. §809.81(8) and §809.86.
Argument is required.
The Argument section should present each of the reasons you think the circuit court decided your case incorrectly. You may have one reason or several reasons. You should list each reason as a one-sentence "argument heading" followed by several paragraphs explaining why you should win on that point. Your Argument should refer to case law, statutes, constitutional provisions, and/or other regulations that support your point. The Brief Assistant will direct you to websites where you can find these legal resources. Your argument should be brief and clear. It should incorporate facts presented in the Statement of Case and Facts. The Argument section is required by Wis. Stat. §809.19(1)(d). Again note that for certain appeals, the law requires appellate briefs to refer to the parties and other individuals by initials, an appropriate pseudonym, or other designation. See Wis. Stat. §809.81(8) and §809.86.
Conclusion is required.
This section should be very short. It should simply tell the Court of Appeals what you want it to do. For example, you might want the Court of Appeals to reverse the entire circuit court decision or only part of it. You might want the Court of Appeals to send your case back to the circuit court for a hearing or a new trial. Be specific. The Conclusion section is required by Wis. Stat. §809.19(1)(e) .
Appendix documents are required.
An Appendix is a set of documents that are essential to understanding the issues you have raised on appeal. Your Appendix must include a copy of the circuit court decision(s) that you are appealing. If the circuit court explained its decision orally, then your Appendix must also include a copy of the transcript showing the decision. Your Appendix may include additional documents relevant to your appeal, but those documents must be part of the Record on Appeal--that is, documents that were first presented to the circuit court. The Appendix is required by Wis. Stat. §809.19(2) .
