Welcome to the Brief Assistant!

The Brief Assistant WILL:

  • Show you an example of an Initial Brief, Response Brief, and Reply Brief for the Wisconsin Court of Appeals.
  • Explain the different sections of these briefs.
  • Direct you to websites where you can find cases, statutes, and other forms of law to cite in your briefs. Click the Legal Resources tab in the navigation bar above.
  • Help you draft a correctly-formatted Initial Brief, Response Brief, or Reply Brief that you can download, save, fill out, edit, and then file in your appeal.

The Brief Assistant will NOT:

  • Generate a completed brief for you. You will need to fill out the various sections of your brief and complete additional steps before you can file it with the Wisconsin Court of Appeals.
  • Do legal research or tell you what law applies to your case.
  • Read Wis. Stat. §809.19 for you. Users are responsible for reading and complying with §809.19, which governs the form, contents, and filing of appellate briefs.

Disclaimer:

The Brief Assistant does NOT provide legal advice. The Appellate Practice Section strongly urges parties to an appeal to retain a lawyer to represent them. Sometimes that is not possible.

So if you have filed a civil appeal and you cannot afford a lawyer, you may wish to visit the Appellate Help Desk for advice on how to prepare your brief.

If you have filed a criminal appeal and you cannot afford a lawyer, you may be eligible for one through the Wisconsin State Public Defender. For criminal appeals in Milwaukee, call 414-227-4805. For criminal appeals in all other parts of Wisconsin, call 608-266-3440.
